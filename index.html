Incredible Video Games of the 2020s

Elden Ring

Released: February 25, 2022

Platforms: PlayStation 4/5, Xbox One/Series X|S, PC

Elden Ring is like if Dark Souls chugged five energy drinks, read a thousand pages of fantasy lore, and decided to emotionally destroy you in the most beautiful way possible. It throws you into a world that’s equal parts majestic and nightmarish, says “good luck, idiot,” and then watches gleefully as a crab the size of a shed smashes you into paste. But somehow, it’s fun? Like, painfully, addictively fun—every boss fight is a high-stakes therapy session, every new area looks like a painting made by someone with unresolved trauma, and the moment you finally take down some demigod who’s been stomping you for three hours straight, you feel like you just won the Olympics in your living room. It’s a game that makes you suffer, but then hands you a glowing sword, a double-jumping goat, and says “now go suffer with style.”

Why Elden Ring is a Incredible Game

  • Vast, interconnected open world
  • Customizable combat and builds
  • Minimal handholding storytelling
  • Critical and commercial success

The Legend of Zelda: Tears of the Kingdom

Released: May 12, 2023

Platform: Nintendo Switch

Tears of the Kingdom is like if Breath of the Wild got a PhD in mad science and then built a jet-powered death machine out of soup pots and optimism. This game doesn’t just let you explore—it practically dares you to break it in the coolest way possible. Want to glue rockets to a minecart and fly into the sky while fighting a robot with a stick? Go nuts. TOTK gives you powers that would make Tony Stark jealous and a playground so massive it makes other open-world games feel like theme park rides with broken tracks. There’s a raw, beautiful chaos to everything: one minute you’re gliding peacefully through sun-drenched skies, the next you’re tumbling into the Depths with half a heart and zero regrets. It’s not just a sequel it’s alchemy, fusing exploration, invention, and raw emotional wonder into something that feels like the wildest Zelda fever dream… and somehow makes it work perfectly.

Why Tears of the Kingdom is a Incredible Game

  • Creative open world with vertical exploration
  • Engaging puzzles
  • Strong Zelda narrative
  • Massive fanbase and praise

Baldur's Gate 3

Released: August 3, 2023

Platforms: PC, PlayStation 5

Baldur's Gate 3 is amazing. Every choice matters in this very immersive game, which is enhanced by its deep plot, nuanced characters, and significant player choices. The turn-based fighting mechanism in the game provides strategic difficulties that incentivize careful preparation and ingenuity. Baldur's Gate 3 is most definetely a must-play for both RPG fans and those who are new to the genre since it blends traditional role-playing aspects with contemporary gameplay improvements. It boasts gorgeous graphics, large environments, and multiplayer options that allow friends to join the adventure.

Why Baldur's Gate 3 is a Incredible Game

  • Player-driven narrative
  • Turn-based tactical combat
  • Faithful D&D adaptation
  • Multiplayer support

Cyberpunk 2077

Released: December 10, 2020

Platforms: PS4/5, Xbox One/Series X|S, PC

Cyberpunk 2077 is an open-world RPG developed by CD Projekt Red. When Cyberpunk 2077 first dropped, oh boy, it was a wild ride — the game launched with a ton of bugs, glitches, and performance issues that had fans scratching their heads and rolling their eyes. Crashes, weird NPC behaviors, and broken quests made some players hesitant to jump in. But hold up, don’t count it out just yet! CD Projekt Red hustled hard, pouring in relentless patches and updates that transformed Night City into the dazzling, neon-soaked cyberpunk playground we all dreamed of. The level of detail is staggering — from the rain-soaked streets to the pulse-pounding soundtrack, and the deeply personal, branching storylines that react to your every choice. Now? Cyberpunk 2077 is an absolute beast — with its sprawling open world, mind-blowing story, and insane character customization options, it’s carved its place as one of the best games of this decade. The immersive gameplay, gripping missions, and next-gen visuals make it a must-play for any gamer hungry for a futuristic adventure. Seriously, if you haven’t dived back in, you’re missing out on a cyberpunk revolution!

Why Cyberpunk 2077 is a Incredible Game

  • Massive open-world experience
  • Multiple story outcomes
  • Next-gen visuals and updates
  • Immersive cyberpunk feel

Spider-Man 2 (PS5)

Released: October 20, 2023

Platform: PlayStation 5

Spider-Man 2 expands the open world, building on the popularity of its predecessors. A sequel like Spider-Man 2 doesn't only pose the question, "Hey, what if we made the first game again but bigger?" "Let's web-sling so hard we dislocate a shoulder," it says. This game is a tribute to Spider-Man that has been tossed through your window and duct-taped to a brick. The battle feels like Arkham after three shots of espresso, the swinging is more fluid than my Sunday brain, and the city? Oh, it's New York, darling - two boroughs now, with no tolerance for traffic regulations. Like emotional Pokémon cards, you can switch between Miles Morales and Peter Parker, and it has significant meaning. The story slaps harder than Aunt May with a frying pan — there’s drama, heart, and moments where you actually care about what's going on. And the villains? Chef’s kiss. Kraven the Hunter is an absolute menace, Venom is a gooey nightmare with voice lines that could rupture your spine, and even the smaller villains get their moments to shine like they’re competing in a WWE tournament for evil dudes with tragic backstories.

Why Spider-Man 2 is a Incredible Game

  • Best-in-class web-swinging and combat
  • Play as Peter and Miles
  • Great storytelling and villains
  • Visually stunning NYC
